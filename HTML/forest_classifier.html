using GIS to see forests

Developing a Tree Canopy Classification Model Using the 2016 National Agricultural Aerial Imagery Program (NAIP)

what is structure from motion?

Structure from motion (SFM) is a photogrammetry technique used for estimating 3D objects from overlapping image sequences. Combining images with spatial information allows for the creation of highly-accurate geospatial products. Whereas commercially available satellite products provide image resolutions in the tens of meters, SFM can provide resolutions measured in centimeters. This enables the creation of high quality data such as orthographic images, digital elevation models (DEMs), and digital surface models (DSMs), which serve as the foundation for environmental remote sensing analysis.

This project was conducted on a small-scale agroforestry plot in the town of Naro Moru in central Kenya. The purpose of the project was to use drone imagery and structure from motion (SFM) to produce a variety of geospatial data products that could be used by the landowner for agricultural and land-use planning purposes.
