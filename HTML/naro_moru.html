Agroforestry Mapping Using Structure From Motion in Naro Moru, Kenya

what is structure from motion?

Structure from motion (SFM) is a photogrammetry technique used for estimating 3D objects from overlapping image sequences. Combining images with spatial information allows for the creation of highly-accurate geospatial products. Whereas commercially available satellite products provide image resolutions in the tens of meters, SFM can provide resolutions measured in centimeters. This enables the creation of high quality data such as orthographic images, digital elevation models (DEMs), and digital surface models (DSMs), which serve as the foundation for environmental remote sensing analysis.

This project was conducted on a small-scale agroforestry plot in the town of Naro Moru in central Kenya. The purpose of the project was to use drone imagery and structure from motion (SFM) to produce a variety of geospatial data products that could be used by the landowner for agricultural and land-use planning purposes.

survey data

ODM provides the ability to conduct accuracy assessments of the imagery collection process. As can be seen from the image below, highly variable areas such as those with large amounts of vegetation require imagery to be captured from multiple angles. A lack of sufficient angles in some areas leads to a low density in the point cloud computation. Therefore, it is essential to capture multiple flight paths and multiple image angles when modeling areas with large amounts of vegetation.

GPS/GCP error details

RMSE or root mean square error is a common statistical measurement that represents how concentrated a dataset is around the line of best fit. In general, a value between 0.2 and 0.5 shows that the model can relatively predict the data accurately. The table below shows an acceptable RMSE value for x-y values, however a slightly higher error in z values. This can easily be rectified with the use of ground control points (GCPs) which were not included in this study.

results

Utilizing drone imagery and SFM, I was able to provide the land owner with several highly accurate data products. The ortho-imagery and digital surface models that were created allowed the owner to successfully create a plantation forestry plan suited to their land use needs. This data could also be used for further environmental analysis including incident solar radiation and hydrological analysis, which are both critical for plantation forestry and land management activities.
